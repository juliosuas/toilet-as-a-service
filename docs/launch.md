## One-line description

An interactive product satire that turns human urgency into measurable ad inventory.

## Media description

Toilet as a Service imagines a network of clean urban restrooms where one fixed sponsor message funds four minutes of access. The polished interface, operating metrics, and business model are fictional; the critique of attention-funded infrastructure is not.

## Publishing notes

- Use the live URL without a manual attachment when the platform renders Open Graph previews correctly.
- If uploading media manually, attach `public/og.png` for the clearest premise-first card, `public/taas-unit-street-blue.webp` for city context, or `public/taas-access-terminal-blue.webp` for a product-detail post.
- Lead with the premise; disclose the satire immediately after the product line.
- Do not describe TaaS as an operating restroom network.
- Link the press room for longer coverage: https://toilet-as-a-service.juliosuas.chatgpt.site/press
- Product and launch materials are intentionally English-only.

## Launch sequence

1. Publish the primary post on X with the generated link preview.
2. Follow with the street-deployment image and the alternate post 12–24 hours later.
3. Publish the media description on LinkedIn with the access-terminal image.
4. Submit the live experience to relevant design, satire, and indie-web communities with an explicit “interactive satire” label.
5. Reply with the press-room link when someone asks for assets or context.

The site includes one-click actions for X, LinkedIn, and Reddit. Final submission always remains under the visitor’s control.
